    \section{Supporting relationships}
    The mass-energy equivalence is described by the famous equation

    \begin{equation}
        E=mc^2
    \end{equation}

    \noindent discovered in 1905 by Albert Einstein. 
    In natural units ($c$ = 1), the formula expresses the identity

    \begin{equation}
        E=m
    \end{equation}
